# X / Twitter Draft V2: A Simple Earnings Season Workflow

Platform: X / Twitter

Language: English

Suggested hook:

Earnings season punishes fast reactions. Use AI to check the facts before you let the headline make the decision for you.

Thread draft:

1/ Most investors use AI backwards during earnings season.

They ask:

"Should I buy after this report?"

Better question:

"What actually changed?"

2/ AI is not useful because it sounds confident.

AI is useful when it can check:

- earnings
- expectations
- price reaction
- valuation
- news
- calendar
- historical moves

Data first.

Opinion second.

3/ That is why Longbridge Skill is interesting.

It lets an AI agent work with market data instead of only general knowledge.

And Longbridge CLI gives you the repeatable tool layer:

quote, K-line, valuation, financial report, news, finance calendar.

4/ Earnings are not just numbers.

They are expectations meeting reality.

A company can grow and still disappoint.

A company can miss one line and still improve where it matters.

The headline rarely tells the full story.

5/ My basic earnings workflow:

Step 1:

Pull the latest quote.

Not to chase the move.

To understand how the market is reacting right now.

6/ Step 2:

Compare actual results with expectations.

Revenue.

Profit.

Margins.

Guidance.

Key business lines.

Do not judge the report before knowing what the market expected.

7/ Step 3:

Break down the business.

Was growth broad?

Was it driven by one segment?

Did margins improve?

Did guidance change?

Did management sound more confident or more cautious?

8/ Step 4:

Check the K-line.

Not because charts predict the future.

Because context matters.

Was this move normal for the stock?

Was volume unusual?

Did the move reverse?

9/ Step 5:

Update valuation.

A stock after earnings is not the same stock as before earnings.

Price changed.

Expectations changed.

Sometimes the story changed.

Your valuation check should change too.

10/ Step 6:

Read news and calendar.

Sometimes the earnings move is not only earnings.

It may be guidance.

It may be regulation.

It may be sector news.

It may be an upcoming event.

11/ Step 7:

Separate facts from opinions.

Facts:

- revenue changed
- guidance changed
- price moved
- valuation moved
- calendar event is coming

Opinions:

- market overreacted
- stock is cheap
- story is broken

Keep them separate.

12/ Prompt you can try:

"Use Longbridge Skill or Longbridge CLI to check the latest quote, K-line, valuation, financial report, news, and finance calendar. Summarize what changed after earnings. Do not give a buy or sell recommendation. List risks and follow-up questions."

13/ That prompt is boring.

That is why it works.

It forces the AI to slow down.

It forces data before opinion.

It turns earnings season from a reaction game into a research process.

14/ The best use of AI in investing is not prediction.

It is preparation.

Less headline panic.

More context.

Less "what should I do?"

More "what do I still need to check?"

15/ Most retail investors do not need a louder opinion.

They need a cleaner workflow.

Longbridge Skill gives AI the market connection.

Longbridge CLI makes the checks repeatable.

You still make the decision.

But you make it with better inputs.

16/ Earnings season is hard because emotions move faster than research.

I get it.

The headline hits.

The price moves.

Everyone has a take.

At minimum, use AI to check the facts before you join the reaction.
